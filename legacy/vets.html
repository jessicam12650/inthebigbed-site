Dog first aid guide

What to do in a dog emergency — while you get to a vet. Always call Vets Now on 0151 733 4444 first.

🌡️
Heatstroke
Move to shade immediately. Pour cool (not cold) water over neck, armpits and groin. Fan them. Offer small sips of water. Never use ice.
Get to a vet immediately — heatstroke can be fatal within minutes.
😮
Choking
Look in the mouth — only remove an object if you can clearly see it. Do not do blind finger sweeps. Hold dog upside down if small. Give 5 firm back blows between shoulder blades.
Call the vet immediately even if you dislodge the object.
☠️
Suspected poisoning
Do not induce vomiting unless told to by a vet. Note what they may have eaten and when. Keep them calm and still. Common dangers: grapes, xylitol, chocolate, ibuprofen, slug pellets.
Call the vet immediately — bring the packaging if you can.
⚡
Seizures
Do not restrain them. Clear the area of anything they could hurt themselves on. Time the seizure. Keep the room dark and quiet. Do not put your hands near their mouth.
If seizure lasts more than 5 minutes — emergency vet immediately.
🩸
Bleeding
Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth. Do not remove the cloth — add more on top if needed. Keep the wound elevated above heart level if possible. Keep the dog calm and still.
Severe or arterial bleeding — emergency vet immediately.
💧
Drowning / near-drowning
Hold the dog upside down briefly to drain water from lungs. Lay on their side. Check breathing. If not breathing — begin rescue breathing. Keep them warm.
All near-drowning dogs need a vet check even if they seem fine.
